<p><br /><strong> Alan Turing &minus; Timeline</strong></p>
<p><br /><strong>1912 (23 June):</strong> Birth, Paddington, London</p>
<p><br /><strong>1926-31:</strong> Sherborne School</p>
<p><br /><strong>1930:</strong> Death of friend Christopher Morcom</p>
<p><br /><strong>1931-34:</strong> Undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge University</p>
<p><br /><strong>1932-35:</strong> Quantum mechanics, probability, logic. Fellow of King's College, Cambridge</p>
<p><br /><strong>1936:</strong> The Turing machine, computability, universal machine</p>
<p><br /><strong>1936-38:</strong> Princeton University. Ph.D. Logic, algebra, number theory</p>
<p><br /><strong>1938-39:</strong> Return to Cambridge. Introduced to German Enigma cipher machine</p>
<p><br /><strong>1939-40:</strong> The Bombe, machine for Enigma decryption</p>
<p><br /><strong>1939-42:</strong> Breaking of U-boat Enigma, saving battle of the Atlantic</p>
<p><br /><strong>1943-45:</strong> Chief Anglo-American crypto consultant. Electronic work.</p>
<p><br /><strong>1945:</strong> National Physical Laboratory, London</p>
<p><br /><strong>1946:</strong> Computer and software design leading the world.</p>
<p><br /><strong>1947-48:</strong> Programming, neural nets, and artificial intelligence</p>
<p><br /><strong>1948:</strong> Manchester University, first serious mathematical use of a computer</p>
<p><br /><strong>1950:</strong> The Turing Test for machine intelligence</p>
<p><br /><strong>1951:</strong> Elected FRS. Non-linear theory of biological growth</p>
<p><br /><strong>1952:</strong> Arrested as a homosexual, loss of security clearance</p>
<p><br /><strong>1953-54:</strong> Unfinished work in biology and physics</p>
<p><br /><strong>1954 (7 June):</strong> Death (suicide) by cyanide poisoning, Wilmslow, Cheshire.</p>

It is played with three people, a man (A), a woman (B), and an interrogator (C) who may be of either sex. The interrogator stays in a room apart front the other two. The object of the game for the interrogator is to determine which of the other two is the man and which is the woman. He knows them by labels X and Y, and at the end of the game he says either "X is A and Y is B" or "X is B and Y is A." The interrogator is allowed to put questions to A and B... We now ask the question, "What will happen when a machine takes the part of A in this game?"
